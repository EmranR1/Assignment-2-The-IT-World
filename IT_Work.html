Darren kitchen

Darren kitchen founded, hosts and produces the hacking and computer security show Hak5, is the author of several books related to hacking such as ‘USB Rubber Ducky: A Guide to Keystroke InjectionAttacks’ (credit ebook) and creator and co-creator of a variety of hacking tools such as the USB Rubber Ducky. Kitchen’s work mainly relates to hacking and computer security, be it as a developer or public image.  

Kitchen’s interactions tend to be with the general public through Hak5 and social media, although his target audience is more specifically those with interests in computers. He does also however sell a variety of hacking related items through Hak5 to customers, and interacts with other computer security specialists to create these items.  

Kitchen spends a large amount of time hosting Hak5, however as of February he has been hacking across Europe meeting fans. As his job relates to creating content and he has a team working with him, his workload varies over time and he can choose to work more or less depending on this. 

For Kitchen, the difficulties he faces now as a public image in the tech space are related more to keeping up with new hacking innovations, and ensuring  the accuracy of the information he provides. Although he is not currently working on any public projects, it can be seen that a great deal of effort has been put into projects such as the wifi pineapple and packet squirrel. It could be argued that this is the most difficult part of his job.

Tarah Wheeler:

Tarah Wheeler is an information security researcher, along with a fellow in New America’s Cybersecurity Initiative. She has worked a variety of job positions in the past including Cyber Project Fellow, Chief Information Security Officer & vCISO Advisor, Head of Offensive Security & Technical Data Privacy, Systems Architect as well as authored books such as ‘Women In Tech: Take Your Career To The Next Level With Inspiring Stories And Practical Advice’, and interviewed by media outlets such as the Washington post. From this it can be seen that her work relates to information security and its protection. She advises companies on digital security measures, researches new and existing security vulnerabilities to develop better security systems, spreads public awareness of digital safety and taught new and existing information security students. Her latest work is on a international cybersecurity capacity building project, which is similar to what she has done in the past.

Wheeler interacts with a variety of people. From her students when teaching or working on projects to other IT professionals when doing research. The people she interacts with can also depend on her target audience, if she has one for a specific project, such as women in her book ‘Women In Tech: Take Your Career To The Next Level With Inspiring Stories And Practical Advice’ or the general public when interviewed about day to day security. She doesn’t usually have clients as she does not offer services so much as employment.

Her current project has her reviewing and developing foreign policies of cyber security in an effort to deescalate and prevent cyber warfare. As part of this a large amount of her time is spent meeting with foreign countries’ governments to discuss policy and future plans. Primarily based in America, and due to the current climate of world safety, it can be assumed that much of this is online rather than in person.

The most challenging aspect of her position stems from the mix of politics and information security. Despite her background in both the crossover can be challenging, as different countries has different views on information security, and potentially different goals as a result. Improving policy while maintaining functionality to the implementation is a large challenge.

Cloud Architect (Lars Clint and Marilag Dimatulac

A role for experienced professionals, who can provide a solution that can meet the needs of the big pictures.

  • Establishing best practices for cloud usage within an organisation.
  • Discussing real business problems and identifying opportunities with cloud technologies
  • Overseeing governors, rules and standards the cloud environment must abide by.
  • IT security tasks to monitor privacy and develop incident response procedures.
  • Plans to mitigate any risks that security and governance might have helped identify.
  • Estimating cloud infrastructure cost and meeting budgets.
  • Design and implement their cloud infrastructure

    • Important skills cloud architects have:

    • Solid understand of cloud computing fundamentals and its advantages and disadvantages which helps with design and allows them to avoid potential "gotcha" moments.
    • Need to understand the options and limitations cloud architectures can provide. Laying the requirements, challenges, business needs, compliances and more.
    • Need to be able to communicate extremely well, listen and understand. Take what you hear and apply it to problem solving. not all problems are technical. The cloud architect is the advocate for cloud adoption. Solutions need to be cost effective and scaleable and give company stakeholders not only what they want but stability and security they need.

    What kinds of people does the IT professional interact with?

    The cloud architect interacts with many IT professionals, clients, investors / the company's stakeholders. And most like with the general public here and there, Whether it is talking about new cloud technology or just sharing their knowledge.

    Where does the IT professional spend most of their time?

    Most of their time is spent talking with stakeholders, creating incident response procedures, designing and implementing a cloud system that meets the business's goals and budget while abiding the cloud environment laws.

    What aspect of their position is most challenging?

    The most challenging aspect is coming up with big solutions that meet the needs of the big picture while discussing business problems and identifying opportunities with cloud technologies.

    Cybersecurity analysts (Sandra and Grant Collins)

    The organisation's defenders. Cybersecurity analysts monitor networks for security breaches and investigate violations. Among many other responsibilities.

    Cybersecurity analysts have a lot of responsibilities including:

    • Analysing security policies and protocols and doing thorough check of audits to determine         weaknesses in the system.
    • They also plan, implement and upgrady different security controls.
    • They conduct and initiate vulnerability scans, risk analysis and security assessments.
    • They conduct annual external and internal audits.
    • They have to respond and anticipate incidents and security alerts happening in the system.
    • They research and recommend appropriate security tools for specific security problems.
    • They manage network prevention and management systems.
    • They maintain and create corporate security policies.
    • They train employees in security awareness and procedures.
    • Manage relationships with outside security vendors.
    • Lots of documenting and assessments.

    Important skills cybersecurity analysts have:

    • Vulnerability testing
    • DLP, ANTI-VIRUS / ANTI-RANSOMWARE / MALWARE
    • TCP/IP STACK, COMPUTER NETWORKING, ROUTING, AND SWITCHING
    • FIREWALL AND IDS / IPS
    • WINDOWS, UNIX, AND LINUX
    • C, C++, PHP, PYTHON, JAVA, BASH, POWERSHELL
    • CLOUD COMPUTING
    • SIEM

    What kinds of people does the IT professional interact with?

    Cybersecurity analysts interact with many different people. They train employees in security procedures and awareness while also managing relationships with outside security vendors. They even talk to clients or investors. Possibly the public as well if they are demonstrating something or have to give explanation for a possible attack on the company and how they are dealing with it.

    Where does the IT professional spend most of their time?

    A Cybersecurity analyst spends most their time behind a computer doing things such as conduction and initiating vulnerability scans, risk analysis and security assessments among other responsibilities however still a fair portion of their time is spent in training employees in security awareness and procedures to avoid having the employees fall victim to different attacks such as phishing and so on.

    What aspect of their position is most challenging?

    As technology is constantly changing the most challenging aspect is deploying new IT initiatives and security measures for Cybersecurity analysts. sources.

    Paul’s IT Professionals:

    Network Engineer

    What kind of work is done by the IT professional? 

    Network engineers are technical experts who specialise in configuring computer networks, maintaining them, and providing technical support and insuring that the network is optimized and functioning as intended. They are the “Bread and butter” of IT professionals when it comes to networks and connections. They troubleshoot a lot of problems by analysing a clients network to find vulnerabilities or issues that need to be addressed such as an unopened port that can be exploited or a router that is assigning the wrong DHCP range for example.

    Some of the main duties a network engineer is responsible for:

    • Maintaining computer networks, 
    • Maintaining hardware, 
    • Upgrading software and related systems
    • Disaster recover and ensuring data is protected
    • Replacing and upgrading faulty network hardware components 

    What kinds of people does the IT professional interact with?

    They are part of an organisation Information and Technology team, Network Engineers work very closely alongside Business Analysts, Network Architects and IT Managers. The network engineer is the specialist when it comes to setting up and maintaining networks, they ensure that everything is up to date and up and running. There networking analytical skills are very important for providing valuable information and reports to the other sections where they do not have as much of a comprehensive understanding of the science behind the network.

    Where does the IT professional spend most of their time?

    Generallythey spend most of there time on a computer with administrative privileges they are able to change, fix and upgrade many features of the network from there desk or even troubleshooting problems which can be very time consuming. However they need to sometimes lift heavy items like servers or plug in cables to routers or computers etc for example ethernet cables or other technical skills that will effect the network.they even spend a lot of there time in a even a server room of an organisation, if they are doing a server upgrade

    What aspect of their position is most challenging?

    Maintenance on the production network can be the most challenging as networkchuck stated that the network can involve a lot of trouble shooting and sometimes the problem may take longer than usual and not go as planned. Staying up for hours and doing a lot of overtime as you cant just call a specialist to fix it. The network engineer is the specialist so they are the backbone of the organization when it comes to the network and connections. They are essential and sometimes they have to put a lot of work in to get things back up and running as soon as possible as time is money for an organization. A challenging think in this network field is troubleshooting and a lot of it. Also networking is constantly evolving so the networks engineers have to constantly stay up to date on the procedures and protocols which can be very time consuming constantly learning whilst working.
